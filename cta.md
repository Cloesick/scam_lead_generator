To make your campaign for mortgage recovery and scam remediation effective, your Call to Action (CTA) must shift from generic commands to benefit-driven outcomes. Research indicates that using a generic "Submit" button can cause a 3% abandonment rate; Instead, CTAs should clearly promise the specific goal the user is trying to reach.

Below are campaign-ready CTAs categorized by their psychological trigger and intended platform placement.

1. Benefit-Driven CTAs (High Conversion)
These focus on the ultimate outcome: regaining the ability to own or move house. Using "you" language directly addresses the visitor's pain points.

"Restore My Mortgage Eligibility"

"Recover My Home's Value"

"Fix My Credit Status Now"

"Check My Eligibility for Removal"

2. Action-Oriented CTAs (High Intent)
These are best for users who are already aware they have a problem (like a CIFAS marker) and are looking for a professional to solve it.

"Clear My CIFAS Marker"

"Start My Case Review"

"Talk to a Recovery Expert"

Get My Free Assessment

3. Optimized Micro-Copy for Lead Forms
When the user is filling out the form, the "micro-copy" (the small text on or near the button) should reduce anxiety and reiterate the value exchange.

Button Text: "Send My Free Guide to Recovery" (Better than "Download").

Trust Micro-copy: "Your data is secured. We never share your details with third parties."

Instructional Copy: "Complete the fields below to see if your location is eligible for remediation."

4. Platform-Specific Implementation
Meta Ads (Facebook/Instagram): Use the "Learn More" or "Get Quote" button on the ad creative itself, but set the final button inside the Instant Form to "Check My Status" or "Start My Recovery" to drive higher intent.

Google Search Ads: Use the Lead Form extension with a CTA like "Apply Now" or "Get Help" . Ensure the headline of the form mirrors the ad's promise, such as "Rejected for a mortgage? We can help." .

Summary of Strategic Alignment
For this specific campaign, the most effective CTA is "Restore My Mortgage Eligibility" . It directly addresses the "access to mortgages" trigger you identified and provides a clear, positive outcome for the victim.Aligning this copy across your ad, your landing page, and your form button is vital to maintaining the trust of users who have already been misled by previous platforms.
