The Spray Foam Insulation Problem: Market Context

Background and Scale of the Issue:
- Approximately 250,000-300,000 UK properties have spray foam insulation installed
- Government VAT exemptions and grants (including the Green Homes Grant) encouraged uptake
- A 2025 Health and Safety Executive report found that condensation could cause 25% of roof timber to decay within five years if spray foam is applied directly to roof tiles
- The industry remains unregulated for both installation and removal

Why Lenders Reject Spray Foam Properties:

| Lender Concern | Technical Explanation | Impact on Homeowner |
|----------------|----------------------|---------------------|
| Timber Inspection | Spray foam covers roof timbers, preventing visual inspection for rot, woodworm, or structural damage | Surveyors cannot sign off on property condition |
| Moisture Trapping | Foam blocks natural roof ventilation, trapping warm moist air against cold timbers | Creates conditions for condensation, rot, and mould |
| Long-term Damage | Hidden decay can progress undetected for years | Property may have undisclosed structural issues |
| Removal Uncertainty | No standardised removal process or certification | Lenders cannot assess remediation quality |

Major UK Lenders with Spray Foam Restrictions:
- TSB: Complete ban on spray foam properties
- Halifax: Rejection without specialist survey
- Nationwide: Requires removal or detailed RICS survey (£500-£1,500)
- Skipton Building Society: Case-by-case, typically rejected
- Leeds Building Society: Requires removal certification
- 50+ additional lenders with similar policies

Note: LiveMore is currently the only equity release lender that will consider properties with spray foam, and even they require removal within 90 days of loan completion.

WARNING: The Spray Foam Removal Scam Ecosystem

The spray foam removal industry is plagued by fraudulent operators who exploit homeowners' desperation. Understanding this ecosystem is critical for both ethical positioning and regulatory compliance.

Common Scam Tactics in the Industry:

| Scam Type | Description | Red Flags |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| Cold Call Scare Tactics | Unsolicited calls claiming property is a "fire hazard" or "unmortgageable" | No prior contact; pressure to sign immediately |
| Fake Certifications | Promising "instant certificates" or "guaranteed mortgage approval" | No RICS survey; no building control involvement |
| Unnecessary Removal | Claiming all spray foam must be removed when only problematic installations need remediation | No assessment; blanket recommendations |
| Elderly Targeting | Specifically targeting older homeowners (equity release demographic) with fear-based messaging | High-pressure sales; doorstep visits |
| Fraudulent Paperwork | Providing fake completion certificates that lenders reject or flag | Certificates not recognised by major lenders |

What Lenders Actually Require:
1. RICS-qualified structural survey (not just a "certificate")
2. Building control sign-off where applicable
3. Evidence of proper timber inspection post-removal
4. Documentation from recognised/insured contractors
5. In some cases, a retention period before full mortgage approval

Consequences of Fraudulent Certification:
- Property permanently flagged in lender systems
- Homeowner liable for misrepresentation
- Potential legal action from buyer/lender
- Removal work may need to be redone by legitimate contractor
