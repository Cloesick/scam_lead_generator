# Marketing Campaign: Wrongfully Installed Insulation Claims (UK)

> Adapted from high-value home improvement finance claims strategy.  
> Focus: Cavity wall insulation, loft insulation, external wall insulation — wrongfully installed under government-backed or installer-arranged schemes.

---

## CORE STRATEGY PRINCIPLE

Your ads and content should:

1. Mention **insulation type explicitly** (cavity wall, loft, external wall)
2. Mention **government-backed schemes** (ECO, Green Deal, HHCRO, Warm Front)
3. Reference **installer negligence** and **surveyor failures**
4. Highlight **property damage** (damp, mould, structural decay)
5. Reference **legal liability of installers, surveyors & scheme administrators**
6. Imply **high-value remediation claims** (£10,000–£50,000+)

If you don't mention the scheme or insulation type, you'll drown in generic home complaint traffic.

---

## TARGET AUDIENCE

- **Age**: 35–70 (homeowners most affected by ECO/Green Deal-era installations)
- **Location**: England, Scotland, Wales — especially Northern England, Midlands, Wales, Scotland (highest density of cavity wall insulation under government schemes)
- **Property types**: Pre-1930s solid wall, exposed cavity wall properties, stone-built, rendered properties
- **Situation**: Experiencing damp, mould, cold spots, crumbling render, condensation — often years after insulation was installed
- **Financial**: Many had insulation installed free or subsidised — unaware they have a legal claim

We help homeowners who:

### SECTION 2 — WHAT WENT WRONG

Under schemes like ECO, Green Deal and HHCRO, millions of homes received free insulation. But:

- **Surveyors failed** to properly assess whether the property was suitable
- **Installers cut corners** — filling cavities in homes with known exposure risks
- **No follow-up inspections** were carried out
- **Moisture is now trapped** inside walls, causing damp, mould, and structural decay

The installers, surveyors, and scheme funders may all be legally liable.

### Phase 1 (Weeks 1–4): Foundation

- Launch Google Search campaigns (highest intent — people already searching)
- Build and test pre-qualifying landing page

- Launch Facebook/Meta awareness campaigns in high-density areas

  - Independent surveyors

## COMPLIANCE & REGULATORY NOTES

- All advertising must comply with **ASA (Advertising Standards Authority)** guidelines
- Avoid guaranteeing outcomes or specific compensation amounts — use "up to" or "typical" language
- If acting as a claims management company, ensure **FCA authorisation** is in place
- If referring to solicitors, ensure proper **SRA compliance**
- GDPR: All lead data must be collected with explicit consent and stored securely
- Include clear disclaimers on all ads and landing pages
